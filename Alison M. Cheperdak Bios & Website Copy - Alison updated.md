### **A Little More About Me — and What Informs My Approach**

I’m Alison Cheperdak, a bestselling author, internationally certified etiquette coach, and the founder of Elevate Etiquette, a modern consultancy devoted to helping people move through life with confidence, clarity, and grace.  
I’m also an attorney, entrepreneur, and content creator, and a lifelong believer that good manners make life lovelier.  
I grew up in a home where respect, kindness, and table manners were expected, but I never attended cotillion or formal etiquette classes as a child. My interest in etiquette in a traditional sense began when I was twenty-two, newly engaged, and suddenly full of questions.  
*Why is it recommended that mothers don’t host their daughters’ showers? How do you address an invitation to a same-sex couple? Is it rude not to follow certain traditions? (Looking at you, garter toss and cake cutting\!) And how do you tell someone their children aren’t invited without making things awkward?*  
My mom suggested I turn to an etiquette book, and I’m so glad I did.  
I read that first book cover to cover, then another, and another. What began as a way to navigate wedding planning quickly became something much more enduring. Over time, I studied etiquette, executive presence, social graces, and protocol simply because I loved it.  
For a long time, this was something I kept to myself. I never wanted anyone to feel judged or uncomfortable knowing they were sitting across from someone who could spot the difference between an oyster fork and a strawberry fork.  
At the same time, I was building a career that, in many ways, paralleled what etiquette teaches. I worked as a television reporter and anchor for an NBC affiliate in New York, earned my law degree from The George Washington University Law School, and went on to serve across all three branches of the federal government, including in the West Wing of the White House. I later practiced commercial litigation at a large D.C. law firm and served in senior leadership at a national nonprofit.  
Still, I didn’t initially see etiquette as a business, but rather as a subject I couldn’t stop learning about.  
Eventually, I enrolled in formal training in both British and North American etiquette, graduating with merit from The English Manner and Beaumont Etiquette’s Train the Trainer programmes.  
It was a dear friend of mine, Arianne, who encouraged me to share what I had learned in a way that felt kind, modern, and encouraging rather than rigid or judgmental.  
So I did.  
I began sharing small, practical etiquette tips online, and what started as something simple grew quickly into something much bigger.  
Today, I’m grateful to connect with millions across social media, to teach seminars at places like Harvard Law School, the U.S. Congress, and the White House, and to be featured in news outlets including *The Washington Post*, *The Wall Street Journal*, *Fox News*, *Fox Business*, *ABC News*, *Southern Living*, *Vogue*, *Martha Stewart*, *Better Homes & Gardens*, and *Daily Mail*.  
But more than anything, I’m grateful for you.  
This thoughtful, curious, and kind community that believes, as I do, that etiquette isn’t outdated, it’s essential.  
Every question you ask, every comment you leave, and every conversation we have is a reminder that good manners aren’t about perfection.  
They’re about the small choices that shape how others feel in your presence.

## **What You’ll Find Inside**

**A thoughtful, modern approach to the everyday moments that shape how you’re perceived—at work, in your relationships, and everywhere in between.**

**Inside, you’ll find guidance on:**

* **First impressions and introductions**  
  **How to enter a room, greet others, and set the tone from the very first moment**  
* **Workplace communication and professional presence**  
  **Navigating meetings, emails, and conversations with clarity and confidence**  
* **The art of conversation**  
  **What to say, what to avoid, and how to keep interactions flowing naturally**  
* **Friendships, family, and social dynamics**  
  **Handling sensitive situations with thoughtfulness and ease**  
* **Dining etiquette and entertaining**  
  **Feeling confident at the table, whether in a professional or social setting**  
* **Modern manners for everyday life**  
  **The small, often overlooked details that make a lasting impression**
