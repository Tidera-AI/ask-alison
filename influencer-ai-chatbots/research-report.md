# How Top Influencers Use AI Chatbots

## Research Summary Report — April 15, 2026

**Scope**: 27 English-speaking influencers and creators researched across 10 categories and 40 data fields each. 10 Tier A (mainstream recognition) and 17 Tier B (category leaders) creators analyzed.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Most creators now have some form of AI chatbot presence

Of 27 creators researched:
- **21 have active AI chatbot products** (78%)
- **3 had bots that are now inactive/defunct** (Caryn Marjorie, MrBeast, Scott Galloway)
- **3 have no AI chatbot at all** (Gary Vaynerchuk, Justin Welsh, Amy Porterfield — though Porterfield embeds AI assistants inside her course modules)

### 2. The dominant platform is OpenAI's GPT Store — but serious creators build custom

| Platform | # Creators Using |
|----------|-----------------|
| OpenAI GPT Store / Custom GPTs | 11 |
| Custom-built proprietary platform | 8 |
| Delphi.ai | 5 |
| Dexa.ai | 4 |
| ManyChat | 3 |
| Steno.ai | 2 |
| Other (Chat Thing, Notion, Spirito) | 3 |

**Key insight**: GPT Store is the easiest entry point but offers the least differentiation. The highest-revenue AI products (Hormozi's ACQ AI, Robbins' Tony Robbins AI, Manson's Purpose app, Burchard's Brendon AI) are all on custom or specialized platforms, not the GPT Store.

- **Scott Galloway**: Launched AI clone via Spirito.ai and Google Labs, then publicly "killed" his digital twin after 4-6 hours due to ethical concerns about AI companions and youth mental health.

## Platform Ecosystem Map

### Delphi.ai — The "AI Clone" Leader
- **Raised**: $16M Series A (Sequoia + Anthropic's Anthology Fund, June 2025)
- **Creators on platform**: Brendon Burchard, Lenny Rachitsky, Codie Sanchez, Ramit Sethi, Lewis Howes
- **Differentiator**: Voice cloning, revenue sharing, full "digital twin" positioning
- **Pricing**: Platform takes a cut of creator revenue; creators set their own pricing

### Dexa.ai — The "Podcast Knowledge" Platform
- **Raised**: $6M seed
- **Creators on platform**: Andrew Huberman, Rhonda Patrick, Seth Godin, Lewis Howes, Tim Ferriss, Tom Bilyeu
- **Differentiator**: Indexes 120+ podcasts with timestamped citations; knowledge retrieval, not persona cloning
- **Pricing**: Freemium ($10/mo Pro)

### OpenAI GPT Store — The Easy Entry Point
- **Creators using**: Ali Abdaal, Steven Bartlett, Tom Bilyeu, Lewis Howes, Scott Galloway, and many unofficial fan-made bots
- **Limitation**: Low differentiation, requires ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo), limited monetization options, no voice cloning
- **Best for**: Free lead magnets and experimental products

### ManyChat — The DM Automation Engine
- **Creators using**: Jenna Kutcher, Natasha Willis (infrastructure), Russell Brunson
- **Revenue driven**: Kutcher attributes $900K-$1.7M/year incremental revenue to ManyChat funnels
- **Note**: Rule-based automation, not generative AI — but evolving toward "Agentic AI" in 2026

## Strategic Patterns

### Pattern 1: "AI as Premium Upsell"
Creators like Hormozi and Robbins charge premium prices ($99-$299/mo) for AI access positioned as a substitute for expensive 1-on-1 coaching. The AI is trained on proprietary methodologies worth millions.

### Pattern 2: "AI as Course Enhancement"
Porterfield, Gadzhi, and Brunson embed AI tools inside existing paid courses/platforms. The AI increases perceived value and reduces support costs without requiring a standalone product.

### Pattern 3: "AI as Free Lead Magnet"
Abdaal, Bilyeu, and Godin offer free AI tools that capture email addresses or drive traffic to paid products. Low friction, high volume.

### Pattern 4: "AI as DM Funnel"
Kutcher and Willis use ManyChat-powered Instagram DM automation as a scalable, rule-based engagement layer. Not generative AI, but delivers measurable ROI ($1M+/year for Kutcher).

### Pattern 5: "Platform-Distributed Clone"
Rachitsky, Sanchez, and Patrick let platforms (Delphi, Dexa) handle the technology while they provide the content corpus. Low effort, but less control over monetization and user experience.

---

## Implications for Ask Alison

1. **The market is validated**: 78% of researched creators have active AI chatbot products. This is not experimental — it's becoming standard practice for creator businesses.

2. **Proprietary content is the moat**: The most successful products are trained on exclusive, high-value content (consulting data, paid courses, decades of methodology). Generic AI wrappers fail.

3. **Voice is table stakes for premium**: Nearly half of creators offer voice, and the highest-charging products (Robbins, Burchard) all include voice cloning.

4. **Freemium-to-paid is the dominant model**: Free access with email capture, driving into paid courses/communities, is the most common and proven path.

5. **The cautionary tales matter**: Caryn Marjorie (content control), MrBeast (low engagement with fake celebrities), and Galloway (ethical concerns) show real risks that need to be addressed in product design.

6. **Platform choice matters**: Custom-built > Delphi > GPT Store for revenue and differentiation. GPT Store is fine for experimentation but not for serious monetization.

## Implications for Ask Alison (Updated)

1. **The market is validated**: 78% of researched creators have active AI chatbot products. This is not experimental — it's becoming standard practice for creator businesses.

2. **Proprietary content is the moat**: The most successful products are trained on exclusive, high-value content (consulting data, paid courses, decades of methodology). Generic AI wrappers fail.

3. **Voice is table stakes for premium**: Nearly half of creators offer voice, and the highest-charging products (Robbins, Burchard) all include voice cloning.

4. **Freemium-to-paid is the dominant model**: Free access with email capture, driving into paid courses/communities, is the most common and proven path.

5. **The cautionary tales matter**: Caryn Marjorie (content control), MrBeast (low engagement with fake celebrities), and Galloway (ethical concerns) show real risks that need to be addressed in product design.

6. **Platform choice matters**: Custom-built > Delphi > GPT Store for revenue and differentiation. GPT Store is fine for experimentation but not for serious monetization.

7. **"Ask my method" is the killer JTBD**: 18 of 27 creators use this pattern. It's the most validated job-to-be-done for creator AI. Companion/persona bots have a 100% failure rate.

8. **Pricing anchors exist**: $10-20/mo for mid-tier (Manson, Koe), $50-100/mo for premium (Robbins, Burchard), $300/mo for ultra-premium (Hormozi). Price should reflect exclusivity of training data.

9. **Bundling beats standalone for most creators**: 5 of the most successful implementations bundle AI into existing paid products rather than selling it separately. This reduces friction and increases perceived value.

10. **Email capture is non-negotiable**: Even free bots should capture emails. Abdaal's GPTs gate behind email signup; this drives $1.5M+/year in downstream course revenue.
